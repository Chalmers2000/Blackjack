📖 How to Play

The Objective

Baccarat is a card game between two hands — the Player and the Banker. You bet on which hand will have a total closest to 9, or whether they'll tie.

How to Bet

  • Pick a side: Player, Banker, or Tie
  • Click chips to set your wager (clicks stack)
  • Press Deal to play the round

Card Values

  • Ace = 1 point
  • 2–9 = face value
  • 10, J, Q, K = 0 points

Hand total = sum of cards, keep only the last digit. For example: 7 + 8 = 15 → total is 5.

Naturals

If either hand totals 8 or 9 after the first two cards, it's called a "natural" and the round ends immediately — no more cards are drawn.

Third-Card Rules

If no natural occurs, the game may deal a third card following fixed rules (you don't choose — it's automatic):

  • Player draws on 0–5, stands on 6–7
  • Banker follows a more complex tableau based on the Player's third card (see below)

Banker Tableau

When Player drew a 3rd card, Banker acts based on that card's value:

Payouts

Player wins1 : 1
Banker wins0.95 : 1 (5% commission)
Tie8 : 1

On a tie, Player and Banker bets push (returned, no win or loss). Tie bets lose on non-tie outcomes.

House Edge

Banker bet1.06%
Player bet1.24%
Tie bet14.4%

Banker has the best odds. Tie pays well but has a very high house edge.
